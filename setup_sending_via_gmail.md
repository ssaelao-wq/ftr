# **Setup Guide: Sending File via Google Workspace**

This document outlines the step-by-step setup and code implementation required to generate a dynamic PDF file and securely email it using an official Google Workspace business domain account.

## 

## **0\. Prerequisite Information**

Before configuring your application or writing any backend code, ensure you have gathered the following credentials, access rights, and domain configurations.

### 

### **A. Administrative Access**

* **Google Workspace Admin Console:** You (or your IT Administrator) must have access to [admin.google.com](https://admin.google.com) to manage API permissions, enable Domain-Wide Delegation, or generate App Passwords.

* **Google Cloud Platform (GCP):** Access to [console.cloud.google.com](https://console.cloud.google.com) under your company's organization folder to create projects and manage API credentials.

### 

### 

### **B. DNS Authorization (Deliverability Prerequisites via Cloudflare)**

To prevent your automated emails from ending up in your clients' spam folders, you must configure DNS records. **These records are published in your Cloudflare DNS settings, but some values are fetched from Google Workspace.**

#### **1\. SPF (Sender Policy Framework)**

* **What it does:** Tells receiving mail servers which IP addresses/services are authorized to send emails on behalf of your domain.

* **Where to configure:** Cloudflare Dashboard.

* **Step-by-Step Setup:**

  1. Log into your **Cloudflare Dashboard**.

  2. Select your company's domain from the home page.

  3. In the left-hand sidebar, click on **DNS** and then select **Records**.

  4. Look through your existing records for any TXT records starting with v=spf1.

     * **If no SPF record exists:**

       * Click the blue **Add record** button.

       * **Type:** TXT

       * **Name (required):** @ (This represents your root domain, e.g., yourcompany.com).

       * **TTL:** Auto (Cloudflare default).

       * **Content:** v=spf1 include:\_spf.google.com \~all

       * Click **Save**.

     * **If an SPF record already exists** (e.g., if you already authorize another service like SendGrid, Mailgun, or Shopify): **Do not create a second SPF record.** Instead, edit the existing record.

       * Click **Edit** next to your existing SPF TXT record.

       * Modify its content to merge Google's SPF. Add include:\_spf.google.com directly before the closing tag (e.g., \~all or \-all).

       * *Example merged record:* v=spf1 include:sendgrid.net include:\_spf.google.com \~all

       * Click **Save**.

#### **2\. DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)**

#### **3\. DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance)**

* **What it does:** Tells receiving servers what to do with messages that fail SPF or DKIM checks (monitoring, quarantine, or reject) and specifies where to send reports.

* **Where to configure:** Cloudflare Dashboard.

* **Step-by-Step Setup:**

  1. Create or identify a mailbox to receive your DMARC reports (e.g., dmarc-reports@yourcompany.com). Avoid sending these to your personal inbox, as reports can arrive daily in large volumes.

  2. Go to your **Cloudflare Dashboard** \-\> **DNS** \-\> **Records** page.

  3. Click the blue **Add record** button:

     * **Type:** TXT

     * **Name (required):** \_dmarc (Cloudflare will display this as \_dmarc.yourcompany.com).

     * **TTL:** Auto

     * **Content:** v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@yourcompany.com; pct=100

       * *Breakdown:*

         * p=none: "Monitor mode" (highly recommended starting point). Emails failing validation are still delivered, but Google/other receivers send reports about them.

         * rua=mailto:...: The email address where daily aggregate XML reports will be sent.

         * pct=100: Applies the policy to 100% of emails sent from your domain.

     * Click **Save**.

  4. After monitoring your DMARC reports for a few weeks to ensure legitimate emails (including your new app) are authenticating correctly, you can edit this record in Cloudflare to enforce stricter policies:

     * Change p=none to p=quarantine (moves failing emails to spam/junk folders).

     * Eventually, change p=none to p=reject (completely blocks failing emails).

### 

### **C. Resource & API Limits**

* **Daily Sending Limits:** Google Workspace accounts are limited to **2,000 outbound messages per day** (as opposed to 500 for free Gmail accounts).

* **File Size Limits:** The total size of your email (including headers, body text, and the PDF attachment buffer) cannot exceed **25 MB**.

## **1\. Authentication & Security Configuration (Google Workspace)**

Because this is a company application, security and reliability are paramount. Choose the authentication option that matches your infrastructure capabilities.

### **Option A: Google Cloud OAuth2 (Recommended for Multi-User/On-behalf-of Apps)**

This method registers your application with Google and allows you to programmatically refresh temporary access tokens using a permanent **Refresh Token** without storing any account passwords.

#### **Step 1: Create a Google Cloud Project**

1. Log into the [Google Cloud Console](https://console.cloud.google.com/) using your corporate Google Workspace credentials.

2. If your organization is already set up in GCP, ensure your active Organization is selected at the top left.

3. Click the project dropdown selection modal at the top left and click **New Project**.

4. Input a descriptive project name (e.g., Company-Mailer-App) and assign it to your company’s Billing Account/Organization directory.

		FTR-MAIL

5. Click **Create** and wait a few moments. Once the creation completes, switch to your newly created project.

#### **Step 2: Enable the Gmail API**

1. Navigate to the left-hand sidebar menu, hover over **APIs & Services**, and click on **Library**.

2. Type Gmail API into the search bar.

3. Click on the **Gmail API** entry in the results and click the blue **Enable** button.

#### **Step 3: Configure the OAuth Consent Screen**

1. Go to the sidebar menu: **APIs & Services** \> **OAuth consent screen**.

2. **User Type selection:** \* Select **Internal** (Since you are using a Workspace business domain, choosing Internal restricts access to employees within your organization. This completely bypasses Google's complex public verification process and is the safest option for company-scoped utilities).

   * Click **Create**.

3. **App Information step:**

   * **App name:** Enter a name like Corporate PDF Automated Mailer.

   * **User support email:** Select your administrator email address from the dropdown.

   * **Developer contact information:** Add your business email.

   * Click **Save and Continue**.

4. **Scopes step:**

   * Click **Add or Remove Scopes**.

   * Under "Filter", search for gmail.send.

   * Check the box for the scope: .../auth/gmail.send (Send messages on your behalf).

   * Click **Update** at the bottom, then click **Save and Continue**.

#### **Step 4: Create OAuth 2.0 Credentials (Client ID & Client Secret)**

1. Navigate to the sidebar menu: **APIs & Services** \> **Credentials**.

2. Click **\+ Create Credentials** at the top and select **OAuth client ID**.

3. **Application type:** Select **Web application**.

4. **Name:** Enter a descriptive name like Backend Node Mailer Client.

5. **Authorized redirect URIs:**

   * This step is crucial for retrieving the Refresh Token. Click **\+ Add URI**.

   * Enter the exact Google OAuth Playground address: https://developers.google.com/oauthplayground

   * Click **Create** at the bottom of the form.

6. A popup will appear displaying:

   * **Your Client ID**

		`YOUR_CLIENT_ID_HERE.apps.googleusercontent.com`

* **Your Client Secret**

  `YOUR_CLIENT_SECRET_HERE`

  * Copy both fields immediately and secure them (or download the client configuration JSON). You will need these for your environment configuration.

#### **Step 5: Obtain your persistent Refresh Token**

Because server-to-server OAuth doesn't have a visual UI for developers to manually log in every hour, we extract a permanent **Refresh Token** via the safe, sandbox environment of the Google OAuth Playground:

1. Navigate directly to the [Google OAuth 2.0 Playground](https://developers.google.com/oauthplayground).

2. Look to the top-right corner and click the **Gear (OAuth 2.0 configuration)** icon.

3. Check the checkbox option: **Use your own OAuth credentials**.

4. Paste your newly created **OAuth Client ID** and **OAuth Client Secret** into their respective fields. Leave the other settings at default.

5. Close the settings dropdown overlay.

6. On the left-hand panel (**Step 1: Select & authorize APIs**), scroll down to the bottom. In the input field labeled "Input your own scopes", enter:  
   https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.send

7. Click the blue **Authorize APIs** button.

8. You will be redirected to the official Google Sign-In prompt:

   * Log in using the exact corporate Gmail account you want to send the PDFs from (e.g., notifications@yourcompany.com).

   * Because the app is "Internal" and hasn't gone through public publishing review, you will likely see an "unverified app" screen. Click **Advanced**, and then click **Go to \[Your App Name\] (unsafe)** to proceed safely.

   * Click **Allow** on the permission screen.

9. You will automatically be redirected back to the OAuth 2.0 Playground page with your authorization code loaded into **Step 2: Exchange authorization code for tokens**.

10. Click the blue **Exchange authorization code for tokens** button.

11. The Playground will retrieve the payload. Look at the expanded section below to find your **Refresh token** (starts with 1//...).

	`"refresh_token": "YOUR_REFRESH_TOKEN_HERE"`

12. Copy this string carefully. This token never expires unless you manually revoke it from your Google account settings. Save it safely in your .env variables as OAUTH\_REFRESH\_TOKEN.

### 

### **Option B: Service Account with Domain-Wide Delegation (Enterprise Standard)**

The most secure, headless authentication method for system-to-system automation (no user interaction/refresh tokens required).

1. **Create Service Account:** In your GCP console, navigate to **IAM & Admin \-\> Service Accounts** and create a service account.

2. **Generate Private Key:** Create a new key in **JSON format** for this service account. Download and keep this file highly secure on your server.

3. **Enable Domain-Wide Delegation:**

   * Edit your Service Account, view its details, and copy its numeric **Unique ID (Client ID)**.

   * Go to your [Google Workspace Admin Console](https://admin.google.com) \-\> **Security** \-\> **Access and data control** \-\> **API controls** \-\> **Manage Domain Wide Delegation**.

   * Add a new client, paste your Client ID, and authorize the following OAuth Scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.send.

4. **Impersonation:** In your backend code, authorize using the Service Account JWT and specify the official email address (e.g., billing@yourcompany.com) you wish to impersonate.

### 

### **Option C: App Passwords (Recommended ONLY for Prototyping)**

A fallback if your IT administrator blocks GCP API access, though less secure than Option A or B.

1. **Enable MFA:** Ensure 2-Step Verification is enabled on the official sending Workspace account.

2. **Generate Password:** Go to [My Account](https://myaccount.google.com) \-\> **Security** \-\> **2-Step Verification** \-\> **App passwords**.

3. **Generate:** Select "Other (Custom name)", name it (e.g., "NodeJS PDF Mailer"), and copy the 16-character password to your server's .env variables as EMAIL\_PASS.

## **2\. Server Implementation (Node.js Blueprint)**

The backend constructs the PDF entirely in-memory as a binary stream/buffer to avoid temporary disk storage management on your server.

### **Required Dependencies**

Initialize your project and install the following packages:

`npm install nodemailer pdfkit dotenv googleapis`

### **Environment Configuration (.env)**

Store secrets away from your code repository.

`# Shared Send Address`  
`EMAIL_USER=official-sender@yourcompany.com`

`# --- Configuration for Option A (OAuth2 - Recommended) ---`  
`# OAUTH_CLIENT_ID=your_gcp_client_id_here.apps.googleusercontent.com`  
`# OAUTH_CLIENT_SECRET=GOCSPX-your_gcp_client_secret_here`  
`# OAUTH_REFRESH_TOKEN=1//your_persistent_refresh_token_from_playground`

`OAUTH_CLIENT_ID=YOUR_CLIENT_ID_HERE.apps.googleusercontent.com`  
`OAUTH_CLIENT_SECRET=YOUR_CLIENT_SECRET_HERE`  
`OAUTH_REFRESH_TOKEN=YOUR_REFRESH_TOKEN_HERE`

`# --- Configuration for Option B (Service Account JSON - Enterprise) ---`  
`# GOOGLE_APPLICATION_CREDENTIALS=path/to/service-account-key.json`

`# --- Configuration for Option C (App Password - Legacy) ---`  
`# EMAIL_PASS=xxxx xxxx xxxx xxxx`

### **Application Code**

`const nodemailer = require('nodemailer');`  
`const PDFDocument = require('pdfkit');`  
`const { google } = require('googleapis');`  
`require('dotenv').config();`

`/**`  
 `* Generates a PDF in-memory and returns it as a Buffer.`  
 `* This prevents temporary file management issues on your server.`  
 `* @returns {Promise<Buffer>}`  
 `*/`  
`function generatePDFBuffer() {`  
    `return new Promise((resolve, reject) => {`  
        `const doc = new PDFDocument();`  
        `const buffers = [];`

        `// Collect binary data chunks as they are written`  
        `doc.on('data', buffers.push.bind(buffers));`  
          
        `// Resolve the entire Buffer once the document finishes writing`  
        `doc.on('end', () => {`  
            `const pdfBuffer = Buffer.concat(buffers);`  
            `resolve(pdfBuffer);`  
        `});`

        `doc.on('error', (err) => {`  
            `reject(err);`  
        `});`

        `// --- Layout Design & Document Content ---`  
        `doc.fontSize(20).text('Official Document', { align: 'center' });`  
        `doc.moveDown();`  
        ``doc.fontSize(12).text(`Generated on: ${new Date().toLocaleDateString()}`);``  
        `doc.text('This is an automatically generated PDF sent from our corporate web application.');`  
          
        `// Finalize writing`  
        `doc.end();`  
    `});`  
`}`

`/**`  
 `* Connects to Google's SMTP/API and transmits the email with the attached PDF.`  
 `* Supports standard OAuth2 (Option A), Service Accounts (Option B), and App Passwords (Option C).`  
 `* @param {string} recipientEmail`   
 `*/`  
`async function sendOfficialEmail(recipientEmail) {`  
    `try {`  
        `// Step 1: Build the PDF document dynamically`  
        `const pdfBuffer = await generatePDFBuffer();`

        `// Step 2: Establish the Transport based on active environment credentials`  
        `let transporter;`

        `if (process.env.OAUTH_REFRESH_TOKEN) {`  
            `// Option A: Standard OAuth2 configuration (Highly Secure & Flexible)`  
            `transporter = nodemailer.createTransport({`  
                `service: 'gmail',`  
                `auth: {`  
                    `type: 'OAuth2',`  
                    `user: process.env.EMAIL_USER,`  
                    `clientId: process.env.OAUTH_CLIENT_ID,`  
                    `clientSecret: process.env.OAUTH_CLIENT_SECRET,`  
                    `refreshToken: process.env.OAUTH_REFRESH_TOKEN`  
                `}`  
            `});`  
        `} else if (process.env.EMAIL_PASS) {`  
            `// Option C: Legacy App Passwords configuration`  
            `transporter = nodemailer.createTransport({`  
                `service: 'gmail',`  
                `auth: {`  
                    `user: process.env.EMAIL_USER,`  
                    `pass: process.env.EMAIL_PASS`  
                `}`  
            `});`  
        `} else if (process.env.GOOGLE_APPLICATION_CREDENTIALS) {`  
            `// Option B: Service Account with Domain-Wide Delegation (Enterprise Standard)`  
            `const key = require(process.env.GOOGLE_APPLICATION_CREDENTIALS);`  
              
            `transporter = nodemailer.createTransport({`  
                `host: 'smtp.gmail.com',`  
                `port: 465,`  
                `secure: true,`  
                `auth: {`  
                    `type: 'OAuth2',`  
                    `user: process.env.EMAIL_USER, // The Workspace account you are impersonating`  
                    `serviceClient: key.client_id,`  
                    `privateKey: key.private_key,`  
                `}`  
            `});`  
        `} else {`  
            `throw new Error('No valid authentication configuration detected in environment variables.');`  
        `}`

        `// Step 3: Define message metadata and attachment parameters`  
        `const mailOptions = {`  
            ``from: `"Official Company Notifications" <${process.env.EMAIL_USER}>`,``  
            `to: recipientEmail,`  
            `subject: 'Your Requested Official Document',`  
            `text: 'Please find attached the official PDF document you requested.',`  
            `attachments: [`  
                `{`  
                    `filename: 'Official_Document.pdf',`  
                    `content: pdfBuffer,`  
                    `contentType: 'application/pdf'`  
                `}`  
            `]`  
        `};`

        `// Step 4: Transmit through Google Workspace`  
        `const info = await transporter.sendMail(mailOptions);`  
        `console.log('Email successfully sent: ' + info.response);`  
        `return info;`

    `} catch (error) {`  
        `console.error('An error occurred during generation or transport:', error);`  
        `throw error;`  
    `}`  
`}`  
`// Execution Export`  
`module.exports = { sendOfficialEmail };`

## **3\. Production Best Practices**

* **Asynchronous Queues:** Generating PDFs and transmitting them via SMTP are inherently slow blocking I/O tasks. Never execute them synchronously inside your API's client-facing request handler. Offload these tasks to a background worker queue (such as BullMQ, RabbitMQ, or Celery) to prevent gateway timeouts.

* **Warm-up and Throttling:** If your Workspace account is brand new, sending 2,000 emails on day one can trigger automated security locks. Gradually increase your sending volumes over a period of 1 to 2 weeks (warm-up) to establish your sending reputation.
